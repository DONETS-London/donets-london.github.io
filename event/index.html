About The Workshop

About The DONetS

The London branch of the Network Science Institute (NetSI) is dedicated to building a strong research community by fostering collaboration and knowledge exchange. Established as a hub for students and scholars, it provides a dynamic space where researchers can connect and work together across geographical boundaries.

To further support this mission, this event aims to bring together PhD students from London and potentially other regions of the UK who are interested in network science and its real-world applications. The goal is to foster an inclusive and dynamic space where students can present their research, exchange ideas, and expand their professional networks in a supportive environment.

Beyond providing a platform for PhD students and early-career researchers to present their work, this conference also offers a valuable opportunity for the PhD NetSI cohort to develop essential skills — such as event organization, networking, and academic leadership — that are crucial for a successful career in research.

F.A.Q

  • Do I need to pay to attend the workshop?

    You need to pay nothing at all except attention ;)

  • Can non-PhD students join?

    You can always participate. But to be able to present, you need to make sure that your work is (almost) complete.

  • I am a student without a STEM background, can I still participate?

    Certainly! The goal of this workshop is to offer a venue for students and researchers from different disciplines to collaborate and learn from each other.

  • I am not based in London or the United Kingdom, can I still participate?

    Certainly! But travel and accommodation costs must be covered by you or your institution. Eligible applicants may apply funding via third party institutions. We hope to offer a list of potential sponsors closer to the event dates.
